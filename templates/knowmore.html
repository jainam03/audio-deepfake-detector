🤖About this Platform 🤖

This platform is built on a text-to-speech model known as Tortoise-TTS. Originally designed for speech generation, we reverse-engineered it to identify artificially generated speech i.e., Voice Deepfakes.

Tortoise-TTS

Tortoise-TTS offers impressive capabilities in generating diverse, natural-sounding voices and replicating specific speakers. While powerful, it requires technical expertise and significant computing resources for setup and operation, making it more complex than alternative systems. If your priority is high-quality, customizable speech with voice cloning, and you possess the technical know-how, Tortoise-TTS could be a valuable tool. But for simpler needs or applications requiring real-time speech generation, exploring other options might be more appropriate.

Tortoise-TTS in Deepfake Detection

While primarily a powerful text-to-speech tool, Tortoise-TTS also holds potential for deepfake voice detection. Its creator, James Betker, offers a Python script within the project called "is_this_from_tortoise.py" specifically for this purpose. It analyzes audio for specific characteristics often present in Tortoise-TTS generated speech, offering a high confidence level in identifying such deepfakes. However, it's important to note that deepfake detection is an evolving field, and this script may not be foolproof against all methods or future advancements.

🐢Working of Tortoise 🐢

Acoustic Feature Analysis: Tortoise-TTS can extract various acoustic features from a voice sample, such as pitch, formant frequencies, energy levels, and prosody. These features can then be compared to the acoustic features of known real voices to identify any significant differences. For example, deepfake voices often have unnatural pitch contours or formant frequencies outside the typical range for human voices.

Voice Synthesis: Tortoise-TTS can synthesize speech from a text prompt. This synthetic speech can be used to train deepfake detection models. The deepfake detection models can learn to identify the subtle differences between real voices and deepfake voices by training on a dataset of real and synthetic voices.

🧠Our Custom RNN 🧠

Our custom Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) is designed for voice classification, specifically distinguishing between fake and real voices. This RNN leverages the power of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) units to effectively capture temporal dependencies in audio sequences.

Key Features:

  • Input Processing: The model preprocesses audio data, including resampling, trimming, and flattening, ensuring uniformity in input dimensions.
  • Sequence Padding: To handle variable-length sequences, we employ sequence padding, ensuring consistent input sizes by extending or truncating audio samples.
  • LSTM Architecture: The core of the model consists of LSTM layers, allowing it to effectively learn and remember patterns over extended temporal contexts.
  • Dropout for Regularization: A dropout layer is incorporated to prevent overfitting by randomly dropping connections during training.
  • Classification Layer: The final linear layer performs classification, distinguishing between fake (label 1) and real (label 0) voices.
